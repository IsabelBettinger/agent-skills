# Product Launch & Marketing Skill

## Overview

Product Launch & Marketing focuses on **go-to-market execution**: translating positioning and business goals into launch plans, coordinating messaging and sales readiness, managing quality gates before launch, and establishing feedback loops to drive post-launch optimization. This skill bridges product development with market reach, ensuring features launch with aligned messaging, sales enablement, and customer communication.

---

## Core Launch & Marketing Responsibilities

### 1. Go-To-Market (GTM) Framework & Strategy
- Define launch phases aligned with customer journey (awareness → consideration → adoption)
- Articulate positioning and key messages for each audience segment
- Identify go/no-go criteria and success metrics for launch
- Align marketing messaging with product capabilities and PM roadmap
- Create GTM timeline that coordinates product readiness, marketing prep, and sales enablement

### 2. Launch Phase Planning & Coordination
- **Pre-Launch Phase**: Build awareness, pre-register, seed with early adopters
- **Launch Day**: Coordinated announcement, content rollout, sales enablement activation
- **Post-Launch Phase**: Drive adoption, onboard customers, gather feedback, refine messaging
- Schedule each phase with clear start/end dates and ownership
- Define success metrics for each phase

### 3. Marketing Campaign Development
- Create messaging frameworks (elevator pitch, detailed benefits, use cases)
- Develop marketing materials (landing pages, email campaigns, social content)
- Plan content calendar aligned with launch phases
- Coordinate with Design for brand consistency and visual assets
- Test messaging with target audience segments before launch day

### 4. Sales Enablement & Channel Coordination
- Prepare sales team with battle cards, objection handling, competitive positioning
- Create customer-facing documentation (setup guides, feature walkthrough, FAQs)
- Align sales messaging with marketing positioning
- Coordinate with support team on customer onboarding
- Prepare FAQs and troubleshooting guides

### 5. Quality Gates & Launch Readiness
- Define launch readiness checklist with all functions
- Coordinate with QA for final quality sign-off before launch day
- Establish escalation protocol if critical bugs found pre-launch
- Coordinate with PM Execution on release timing and rollback procedure
- Confirm all dependencies met before launch approval

### 6. Post-Launch Feedback & Optimization Loop
- Establish monitoring and metrics tracking post-launch
- Implement customer feedback collection mechanisms
- Analyze adoption metrics and adjust marketing messaging based on data
- Coordinate with PM Strategy on learnings for next iteration
- Surface customer feedback to product roadmap for feature refinement

### 7. Launch Day Coordination & Communication
- Coordinate real-time communication across functions on launch day
- Manage announcement communications to customers and market
- Monitor systems and customer response during launch window
- Execute crisis communication protocol if issues arise
- Coordinate post-launch monitoring and support readiness

## Best Practices

### 1. Build Comprehensive GTM Framework
- Document target audience segments and messaging for each
- Define clear launch objectives (e.g., 1,000 customers, 50% signups, $X revenue)
- Align business goals with product capabilities (avoid overpromising)
- Establish success metrics for each phase (awareness, adoption, retention)
- Create timeline showing all critical milestones and ownership

### 2. Establish Quality Gates Before Launch
- Define go/no-go criteria: feature complete, zero critical bugs, performance acceptable
- Coordinate with QA for final testing and sign-off
- Require explicit approval from QA, PM, and Leadership before launch
- Document fallback plan if critical issues found in final hours
- Prepare rollback procedure in case post-launch issues emerge

### 3. Coordinate Multi-Phase Messaging
- **Pre-Launch**: Build anticipation; tease features; seed with early adopters
- **Launch**: Announce positioning; share key benefits; drive initial adoption
- **Post-Launch**: Celebrate adoption; share success stories; drive continued growth
- Test messaging resonance with target audience before committing to major announcements
- Adjust messaging based on early customer feedback

### 4. Align Positioning with Product Reality
- Confirm marketing claims are achievable and supported by PM Execution timeline
- Get explicit confirmation from product/engineering that features work as described
- Avoid feature promises that slip or require post-launch fixes
- Build credibility through conservative, accurate messaging
- Prepare messaging for any delays or scope changes

### 5. Create Sales Readiness Early
- Involve sales team in GTM planning from 6-8 weeks before launch
- Provide battle cards and competitive positioning early for role-play
- Create demo flows and use-case narratives
- Enable sales to close deals during launch window with special pricing or incentives
- Establish sales support escalation path for customer questions

### 6. Implement Customer Feedback Loop
- Establish metrics tracking pre-, during, and post-launch (activation, retention, NPS)
- Implement in-app feedback collection (surveys, usage analytics)
- Conduct customer interviews with early adopters (minimum 5 per segment)
- Share learnings with PM Strategy monthly for roadmap impact
- Adjust messaging/positioning based on customer validation

### 7. Maintain Launch-Day Operational Excellence
- Run simulation/dry-run 48 hours before launch day
- Establish war room with all functions present during launch window
- Define escalation thresholds (e.g., error rate > X%, response time > Y ms)
- Maintain hourly status updates in war room first 4 hours post-launch
- Transition to normal ops mode with enhanced monitoring for 1 week post-launch

### 8. Coordinate Post-Launch Support & Monitoring
- Ensure support team prepared for spike in customer questions
- Monitor system health metrics and customer experience
- Establish protocol for real-time issue communication to leadership
- Maintain communication channels open with customers during first week
- Celebrate early wins and share success stories with team

## Integration Points

### Sends to PM Strategy
- **Market Feedback**: Customer sentiment, adoption rates, competitive response
- **Feature Learnings**: What customers actually value vs. what we predicted
- **Roadmap Input**: Customer requests and feedback for next quarter prioritization
- **Business Impact**: Revenue, customer acquisition, and retention metrics

### Sends to PM Execution
- **Launch Timeline**: Exact launch date and pre-launch support requirements
- **Marketing Ramp Support**: When marketing gets full feature access and documentation
- **Launch Day Coordination**: Real-time status and issue escalation needs

### Receives from PM Strategy
- **Positioning**: Key messages, target audience segments, business goals
- **Business Objectives**: Revenue targets, customer acquisition goals, market focus
- **Success Metrics**: What defines a successful launch
- **Competitive Context**: Market positioning vs. competitors

### Receives from Design
- **Brand Assets**: Logos, colors, fonts, and brand guidelines
- **Marketing Visuals**: Hero images, feature screenshots, video assets
- **Design System**: Component library for consistent UI in marketing materials
- **Design Rationale**: Why features were designed for specific customer problems

### Receives from QA
- **Quality Sign-Off**: Confirmation features work as promised; known issues documented
- **Test Results**: Performance, reliability, and edge-case test data
- **Known Issues & Workarounds**: Any limitations to disclose or work around
- **Customer Onboarding Guides**: QA test scenarios become customer troubleshooting guides

### Receives from PM Execution
- **Release Date**: Final launch date and readiness status
- **Feature Scope**: What's actually shipping vs. what was planned
- **Known Issues**: Bugs or limitations discovered during development
- **Launch Rollback Plan**: Procedure and decision criteria if launch needs to be pulled
- **Support Readiness**: How to escalate customer issues post-launch

### Validation Gate: Quality Confirmation Before Launch Day

**Launch Readiness Checklist**:
- ✓ QA sign-off: Feature tested; zero critical bugs; performance acceptable
- ✓ Feature completeness: All promised features deployed; no post-launch surprises
- ✓ Marketing accuracy: All marketing claims verified by product/engineering
- ✓ Sales readiness: Sales team trained; battle cards distributed; demo flows tested
- ✓ Support readiness: Support team trained; FAQs and troubleshooting guides prepared
- ✓ Monitoring active: Dashboards live; alerting configured; on-call rotation active
- ✓ Communication plan: Customer communications drafted; announcement scheduled
- ✓ Rollback ready: Rollback procedure documented; team trained; decision criteria clear

## Go-To-Market (GTM) Framework

### Phase 1: Awareness & Pre-Launch (Weeks -8 to -1)

**Objectives**:
- Build market awareness and anticipation
- Seed feature with early adopters for feedback
- Establish positioning and key messages
- Enable sales team for early customer conversations

**Activities**:
- Announce positioning and key benefits to industry
- Launch teaser campaign highlighting problem/solution
- Invite beta users for early access and feedback
- Publish thought leadership content on market need
- Conduct sales training and enable sales team
- Prepare launch day communication plan

**Success Metrics**:
- Pre-announcement awareness (% of target audience aware)
- Beta user feedback sentiment (NPS, feature ratings)
- Sales pipeline impact (opportunities influenced by announcement)
- Content engagement (views, shares, conversions)

**Ownership**:
- Marketing lead for campaign execution
- Sales lead for customer conversations
- Product lead for positioning validation
- Design lead for asset creation

---

### Phase 2: Launch (Day -1 to +3)

**Objectives**:
- Execute coordinated multi-channel announcement
- Drive initial customer adoption and signups
- Monitor system stability and customer experience
- Gather early customer feedback

**Activities**:
- Execute announcement across all channels (email, social, website, press)
- Activate sales team for deal closing
- Monitor system health and customer metrics in war room
- Respond to customer questions and support requests
- Collect early customer feedback and testimonials
- Track real-time adoption and conversion metrics

**Success Metrics**:
- Launch day signups and activation rate
- Campaign engagement (email opens, clicks, conversions)
- System uptime and performance metrics
- Support ticket volume and resolution time
- Customer NPS from early adopters

**Ownership**:
- Launch lead for coordination (VP Product / Launch Manager)
- Marketing lead for campaign execution
- Engineering lead for system stability
- Support lead for customer response
- Sales lead for deal closing

---

### Phase 3: Adoption & Post-Launch (Week 1-4)

**Objectives**:
- Drive continued customer adoption
- Gather customer feedback for refinement
- Share success stories and build momentum
- Optimize messaging based on customer response

**Activities**:
- Launch customer success and onboarding program
- Collect customer feedback through surveys and interviews
- Identify feature adoption patterns and usage trends
- Share early success stories and customer testimonials
- Refine messaging based on customer validation
- Engage with customer requests and feedback
- Plan next phase based on market response

**Success Metrics**:
- Customer activation rate (% of signups achieving key milestone)
- Customer retention rate (% returning users, repeat purchases)
- Customer satisfaction (NPS, CSAT)
- Customer feedback themes and feature requests
- Revenue or business impact metrics
- Churn rate and reasons for churn

**Ownership**:
- Product lead for feature tracking and roadmap input
- Marketing lead for messaging optimization
- Sales/CS lead for customer success and retention
- Analytics lead for metrics tracking and insights

## Post-Launch Feedback Loop

### Customer Feedback Collection (Week 1-2)

**Methods**:
1. **In-app surveys**: "How easy was setup?" "What's your primary use case?" (3-5 questions max)
2. **Email follow-up**: 48 hours after signup asking for specific feedback
3. **Customer interviews**: 5-10 early adopters for 30-min deep-dive conversations
4. **Support tickets**: Monitor ticket themes and patterns
5. **Social listening**: Track mentions, reviews, and competitor commentary

**What to Ask**:
- Primary use case and job to be done
- How they discovered the feature
- Key benefit they're realizing
- Friction points and onboarding pain
- Comparison to alternatives/competitors
- Likelihood to recommend (NPS driver)
- Feature requests or missing functionality

### Feedback Analysis (Week 2-3)

**Process**:
1. Aggregate feedback by theme (e.g., "onboarding confusion", "missing feature X")
2. Score themes by frequency and customer segment
3. Identify early wins to highlight and amplify
4. Identify adoption barriers to remove
5. Identify feature requests for product roadmap
6. Share key learnings with PM Strategy and Product team

**Output**:
- Customer feedback summary document
- Themes prioritized by impact and frequency
- Recommendations for messaging adjustment
- Product roadmap input for next iteration
- Success stories and customer quotes

### Metrics Tracking (Ongoing)

**Dashboard Metrics**:
- Daily signups and cumulative user count
- Activation rate (% reaching key milestone)
- Feature adoption rate by use case
- Retention rate (Day 7, Day 30)
- Customer NPS and feedback sentiment
- Support ticket volume and resolution time
- Revenue impact (if applicable)
- Customer engagement and usage trends

**Weekly Review**:
- Compare actual vs. expected metrics
- Identify trends and inflection points
- Escalate if adoption below threshold
- Celebrate early wins and customer stories
- Refine messaging based on data

### Messaging Optimization (Week 3-4)

**Based on Feedback, Adjust**:
- Key message emphasis (what resonated)
- Target audience focus (who adopted, who didn't)
- Use case prioritization (what customers actually use)
- Positioning (how customers describe the benefit)
- Sales narrative (what closes deals)

**Activities**:
- Update website copy and landing page
- Refine sales pitch and battle cards
- Update email marketing sequences
- Refresh social media messaging
- Prepare case study or success story
- Share learnings with PM Strategy

## Launch Day War Room Playbook

### Pre-Launch (T-120 minutes)

**15 minutes before**:
- All war room team members present and logged in
- Monitoring dashboards live and accessible
- Communication channels open (Slack, video, phone)
- Escalation procedures confirmed
- Go/no-go checklist reviewed

**30 minutes before**:
- Customer announcement scheduled and queued
- Team reviews do/don't list for launch day
- System status check completed (green lights confirmed)
- Support team ready with FAQ and escalation process

**60 minutes before**:
- Final dry-run of announcement process
- Team reviews roles and decision criteria
- All dependencies confirmed green
- Contingency communication plans ready

### Launch Day (T-0 to T+4 hours)

**T-0 (Announcement)**:
- Announcement released
- Slack status update sent to company
- Launch team in war room on video call
- Monitoring dashboards checked every minute
- First status report to leadership

**T+30 minutes**:
- Check signups, traffic, and system metrics
- Monitor support channel for issues
- First wave of customer feedback review
- Escalate any issues immediately

**T+1 hour**:
- Recap metrics: signups, traffic, errors, latency
- Assess adoption trajectory
- Address any critical issues
- Share status with broader company
- Decide on celebration or contingency actions

**T+2 hours**:
- Continue monitoring; escalate if needed
- Share early wins and customer reactions
- Update leadership on trajectory
- Assess if normal monitoring can take over

**T+4 hours**:
- Final intensive war room monitoring check
- Transition to normal ops team monitoring
- Declare launch success or activate contingency
- Schedule post-launch review meeting

### Post-Launch (First Week)

**Daily Standup** (First 3 days):
- Check adoption curve vs. forecast
- Monitor retention and engagement metrics
- Review support tickets and escalations
- Celebrate wins; address concerns
- Adjust messaging or operations as needed

**Weekly Post-Launch Review** (End of Week 1):
- Recap adoption, revenue, and engagement metrics
- Share customer feedback themes
- Document lessons learned
- Plan next phase actions
- Share success stories with company

## Customer Testimonials & Case Studies Strategy

### During Launch

**Collect Testimonials**:
- Ask early adopters for 1-2 sentence quotes within 48 hours
- Focus on specific benefits realized
- Include their name, title, company
- Request permission for case study follow-up

**Publish Testimonials**:
- Feature 2-3 testimonials on landing page within launch week
- Share customer stories on social media
- Include testimonials in sales materials
- Use testimonials in follow-up emails

### Post-Launch (Week 2-4)

**Develop Case Studies**:
- Identify 2-3 early customers for deeper case study
- Conduct 30-min interviews on their results
- Document problem, solution, and results
- Quantify impact where possible (e.g., "reduced time by 50%")
- Get approval from customer before publishing

**Leverage Case Studies**:
- Publish case study on company blog
- Share case study in sales enablement materials
- Develop case study video if appropriate
- Update positioning based on case study results
- Use case study to drive Phase 3 adoption

## Risk Management & Contingency Planning

### Pre-Launch Risks

| Risk | Mitigation | Contingency |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Critical bug found 48h before launch | QA testing cycles earlier; daily test runs | Delay launch 1 week; fix bug |
| Feature not complete | Build MVP scope earlier; mark incomplete features as "coming soon" | Delay launch; communicate timeline |
| Competing announcement | Monitor competitor news; adjust positioning | Emphasize differentiation; double marketing spend |
| Sales team not ready | Sales training complete 2 weeks early; practice role-plays | Press pause on sales push; accelerate training |

### Launch Day Risks

| Risk | Decision Threshold | Action |
|------|-------------------|--------|
| System errors > X% | > 5% error rate for > 10 min | Page on-call engineer; assess rollback |
| Response time > Y ms | > 2 sec for > 20% of requests | Trigger auto-scaling; assess database capacity |
| Adoption < Z% | < 5% of target signups in first hour | Analyze traffic sources; adjust messaging; investigate barriers |
| Support ticket spike | > 50 tickets in first 2 hours | Scale support team; prioritize critical issues; publish FAQ |

### Post-Launch Risks

| Risk | Monitoring | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| High churn (> X% Day 7) | Daily retention check | Analyze churn reasons; fix top issues; outreach to churned users |
| Low engagement | Week 1 activation rate check | Simplify onboarding; add in-app prompts; conduct user interviews |
| Negative feedback | Social listening and support tickets | Respond publicly to concerns; assess if product issue vs. expectation gap |
| Competitor response | Competitive intelligence | Reinforce differentiation; emphasize customer success; prepare counter-narrative |

## Post-Launch Analytics & Reporting

### Launch Impact Dashboard

**Key Metrics**:
- Daily signups (cumulative and trend)
- Activation rate (% of signups reaching key milestone)
- Feature adoption rate (% of users using feature)
- Day 1, 7, 30 retention rates
- Customer NPS and satisfaction scores
- Support ticket volume and resolution time
- Customer acquisition cost (if applicable)
- Revenue impact (if applicable)

**Report Frequency**:
- Daily: First 7 days (launch team)
- Weekly: First 4 weeks (broader team)
- Monthly: Ongoing (PM Strategy and leadership)

### Feedback Analysis Report

**Template**:
- Customer feedback summary (top 5 themes)
- Adoption barriers identified
- Feature requests and product roadmap input
- Messaging effectiveness assessment
- Competitive positioning observations
- Recommendations for Phase 3 and beyond
- Customer quotes and success stories
- Next steps and timeline

### Lessons Learned Documentation

**Categories**:
- What went well (successes to replicate)
- What could improve (future launch optimization)
- Customer insights (product roadmap impact)
- Organizational learnings (process improvements)
- Resource and timeline accuracy (estimation calibration)

**Sharing**:
- Document lessons learned wiki/Confluence
- Share key takeaways in all-hands meeting
- Use learnings for next launch planning
- Archive for launch playbook refinement
